\section{Welcome to the course!}\label{welcome-to-the-course}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Welcome to the \texttt{inlabru} workshop!
\item
  The aim of this workshop is to introduce you to a range of statistical
  modelling approaches, in particular the temporal, spatial and
  spatio-temporal modelling as implemented in the \texttt{inlabru}
  package.
\item
  Workshop materials are available in the github repository
\item
  ⚠️\textbf{Important!} ⚠️ Please use the instructions provided
  \href{Install_software.qmd}{here} to install and check your
  installation \emph{before} the course start.
\end{itemize}

\section{Learning Objectives for the
workshop}\label{learning-objectives-for-the-workshop}

At the end of the workshop, participants will have an understanding of:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  the motivation for and the challenges of analysing and modelling
  spatial data
\item
  statistical models used to analyse spatial and spatio-temporal data
\item
  the implementation of these models in the \texttt{inlabru} package
\item
  how to independently analyse spatial data with \texttt{inlabru}
\end{itemize}

\section{Intended audience}\label{intended-audience}

The workshop aims to cater for participants with a range of different
backgrounds, who is interested in analysing data with modern spatial and
spatio-temporal statistical modelling approaches.

\section{Prerequisites}\label{prerequisites}

Participants should be familiar with the \texttt{R} environment, and
general statistical approaches for modelling such as regression,
analysis of (co)variance, and generalized linear models.\hspace{0pt}

No knowledge of \texttt{R-INLA} or \texttt{inlabru} is required.
